Neural Manifold Learning for Low-Resource Machine Translation

A. Santomauro · E. Simoni · J. Hassel
Department of Computer Science · University of Eastern Piedmont
Abstract

We present a manifold-aligned encoder that improves translation quality for low-resource language pairs without relying on parallel corpora. By projecting source and target embeddings onto a shared latent manifold, the model exploits geometric structure to bootstrap alignment from monolingual data alone.

1 · Introduction

Neural machine translation has achieved remarkable results on high-resource pairs, yet performance degrades sharply when parallel data is scarce. Prior work addresses this with back-translation and transfer learning, but these methods assume access to a related high-resource pair, which is often unavailable for genuinely under-served languages.

Our contribution is threefold. First, we formalise the alignment problem as manifold matching. Second, we introduce a curvature-regularised objective. Third, we report a 4.2 BLEU improvement averaged across eight low-resource pairs.

2 · Method

Let the source and target embedding spaces be sampled from two Riemannian manifolds. We estimate a diffeomorphism between them by minimising a transport cost regularised by local curvature, encouraging neighbourhood-preserving alignment across languages.
